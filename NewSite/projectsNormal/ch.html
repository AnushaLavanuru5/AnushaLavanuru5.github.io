Community History through AR

Overview

  • Preserving Cultural Significance: This Augmented Reality (AR) application was designed to preserve the historical and cultural significance of Harlem, New York, amidst gentrification.
  • Empowering Residents: The project enables Harlem residents to rediscover their communityâ€™s history by interacting with AR-enhanced narratives of landmarks, historic buildings, and community stories.
  • Inspired by Emoverse Features: The application allows users to leave AR assets, comments, and stories at various locations, fostering a sense of shared history and connection.

The project explores the following questions:

  • How can AR be effectively utilized to preserve community history?
  • What are the key UI/UX design considerations for developing a community-centered AR application?

Tech Stack

  • Programming Languages: Python
  • Libraries: Unity, Blender, Figma
  • Tools: Adobe Illustrator, Balsamiq

Responsibilities

  • Community Research: Conducted formative studies, including field research and archival analysis, to understand Harlemâ€™s historical context and challenges. Collaborated with the NSF Center for Smart Streetscapes (CS3) and the 125th Street Business Improvement District to align the application with community needs.
  • UI/UX Design: Designed multi-layered wireframes using Figma, integrating features such as:
    • Heatmaps inspired by Snapchat, showcasing historical landmarks and storytelling.
    • Interactive maps focused on themes like health, safety, history, and art.
  • AR Features:
    • Built 3D models of significant historic buildings using Blender, enabling users to visualize changes over time.
    • Prototyped AR functionalities in Unity, allowing users to explore historical narratives through their smartphone cameras.
    • Integrated features inspired by Emoverse, where users could leave comments, stories, and AR objects tied to locations.
  • Pilot Study: Conducted a pilot study with 10 participants using the beta mobile application to test AR functionalities and gather feedback for iterative development.

Key Features

  • Heatmap Functionality: Highlighted key landmarks and storytelling elements across Harlem.
  • AR Storytelling: Enabled users to view historical narratives and visual changes by pointing their phone cameras at specific locations.
  • Interactive Maps: Focused on themes such as safety, history, and local art for immersive user engagement.

Future Scope

  • Enhanced User Experience: Refine AR features to improve navigation, accessibility, and storytelling interactions.
  • User Studies: Conduct formal studies with Harlem residents to guide development and ensure the platform reflects their needs.
  • Research Publication: Publish findings on the intersection of AR and cultural preservation to highlight its broader societal impact.

Applications

  • Cultural Preservation: Empower communities to safeguard their history.
  • Education: Create immersive learning tools for schools and cultural institutions.
  • Civic Engagement: Foster stronger connections among residents through shared stories.
