Emoverse

Overview

Emoverse is an Augmented Reality (AR) platform designed to promote emotional well-being among university students. By enabling users to externalize, share, and interact with emotions in a virtual space, Emoverse fosters a sense of community and provides support for addressing academic stress and emotional challenges.

The project leverages asynchronous communication and AR to allow users to create AR objects representing emotions, paired with descriptive text, and share them within a virtual environment. Other users can respond interactively, offering empathy and support.

Tech Stack

  • Platform:Augmented Reality (AR) using tools like Luma
  • Prototype Design: Figma for UI/UX prototyping.
  • Backend & Testing:Email-based wizard-of-oz setup for user interaction testing

Key Features

  • Emotion Externalization: Users create AR objects that symbolize their emotions, accompanied by descriptive text for added context.
  • Interactive Emotion Sharing: AR emotions are placed in virtual spaces, where users can interact with them through actions like touching, poking, and patting.
  • Community Support Threads: Emotions evolve into collaborative emotional journeys as users respond with supportive messages, forming threads of interaction.
  • Asynchronous Communication: Facilitates delayed yet meaningful support exchanges, ensuring accessibility and emotional connection over time.

Project Highlights

  • Reduced Stress and Anxiety: Conducted user studies demonstrating reduced stress, anxiety, and negative moods after engaging with Emoverse.
  • Interactive Prototype Design: Designed an interactive prototype in Figma, incorporating key features like customizable AR objects and personalized text prompts.
  • Community Support Validation: Validated emotional sharing as an effective tool for fostering a sense of community support among participants.
  • Refined UI/UX: Integrated feedback from participants to refine UI/UX and enhance AR-based emotional interactions.

Applications

  • Mental Health: Supports emotional reflection and asynchronous sharing to promote mental well-being.
  • Therapeutic Settings: Enhances access to mental health resources and creates immersive environments for self-reflection.
  • Community Building: Fosters a supportive virtual space for empathy-driven interaction and emotional connection.

Impact

  • Quantitative Results: Participants reported a 50% reduction in negative moods and a 12% decrease in anxiety over 24 hours (as per Figure 5 in user study). Demonstrated the effectiveness of AR in externalizing emotions and facilitating supportive interactions.
